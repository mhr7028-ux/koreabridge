👑 King Sejong's Heritage

Hangeul: Created for Everyone

Invented in 1443 by King Sejong the Great, Hangeul was designed to be so simple that "a wise man can learn it in one morning, and a foolish man can learn it in ten days."

Unlike other complex scripts, Hangeul is phonetic and scientifically structured. Our app simplifies the rules, allowing you to read any Korean poster, store sign, or menu in just 24 hours.

🏢 Busan Stay & Eat (2차 사업 연계)

We recommend affordable guesthouses, local restaurants (Milmyeon, Dwaeji-gukbap), and hot tourist spots curated by local youth.

  • Gwangalli Guesthouse Ocean View: $20/night. Cozy rooms, walking distance to Gwangalli beach.
  • Haeundae Youth Hostel: $25/night. Great community, near Haeundae subway station.
  • Seomyeon Station Coliving Studio: $400/month. Perfect for digital nomads doing a "one-month stay in Busan."
  • Dwaeji-gukbap (Pork Soup): Rich pork broth served with rice. Spot: Ssangdung-i Dwaeji-gukbap.
  • Milmyeon (Wheat Noodles): Refreshing cold noodles, Busan style. Spot: Choryang Milmyeon.
  • Jagalchi Seafood Market: Fresh sashimi and grilled fish at Korea's largest fish market.
  • Haeundae Beach & Blueline Park: Take the Sky Capsule along the scenic coastline.
  • Gamcheon Culture Village: Colorful Lego-like houses stacked on coastal hills.
  • Gwangalli Beach: View the illuminated Gwangandaegyo Bridge and the Saturday Drone Show.
